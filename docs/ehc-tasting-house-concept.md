# EHC Tasting House

## One-line pitch

A permanent EHC-branded tasting room, small event space, and Verified-seal certification office in **Wildau**, 40 minutes by S-Bahn from central Berlin, anchoring European craft hot sauce as a tourism and food-culture category in a post-coal Lausitz region while staying reachable for the carless Berlin producers who power the network.

## Why this reads as eligible to ILB

The funded operation is not "a press council." It is a **commercial hospitality and food-manufacturing business** that happens to be operated by EHC. Three revenue lines, all in eligible WZ 2008 categories:

- **Sector I (Gastgewerbe)**: paid tasting flights, ticketed pairing dinners, private hire for weddings and corporate events
- **Sector G (Trade)**: retail of EHC-Verified sauces and EHC-branded merchandise on site
- **Sector C (Manufacturing)** optional: small co-packing or finishing line for EHC-branded products (gift sets, sampler boxes)

The Verified-seal administration and EHC press operations run from the same building as back-office overhead, not as the funded purpose. This is the same structural move as a brewery taproom: the taproom is the eligible hospitality business, the brewery brand sits behind it.

## Location: Wildau, Landkreis Dahme-Spreewald

Locked 2026-06-22. Reasoning: most EHC-connected producers live in Berlin and don't own cars. The venue needs S-Bahn reach from central Berlin or it doesn't function. Burg/Spreewald is the stronger brand story but operationally unreachable for the actual people who would programme and use the space.

**Why Wildau works**:

- **S-Bahn S46** from Berlin Westend, ~40 minutes to Wildau station. Also S8, S85, RE7. Producers can come by train with a cold-bag and a few bottles
- **9 km from Berlin Schönefeld / BER airport** — international visitors land, hop one S-Bahn stop, arrive at venue
- **In Landkreis Dahme-Spreewald**, fully JTF-eligible at the De-minimis 70% rate
- **TH Wildau** (University of Applied Sciences, ~3,500 students) brings weekday lunch and evening footfall plus a tech/research-curious audience
- **A10-Center** drags regional weekend traffic right past Wildau
- **Dahme riverfront** and Schwartzkopff industrial-heritage quarter give actual atmosphere and event-able locations, not just a dormitory commuter town
- Dataset already shows **8 JTF-funded projects in Wildau** — ILB recognises the postcode

**On the funding rate**: LDS is a D-Area under AGVO (lower regional ceiling than SPN/CB), but **the De-minimis route is independent of the regional aid map**. We target De-minimis at 70%, same path Reuner used 30 km south. The lower AGVO ceiling is irrelevant to this application shape.

**Held in reserve, not primary**: Burg/Spreewald (UNESCO biosphere, real food-tourism flow, dozens of comparable approved grants). Worth considering for a second venue if the first works, or if a destination model becomes more important than producer access. Schlepzig, Lübbenau, Cottbus, Senftenberg, Görlitz also remain options on the same JTF logic.

**One thing to know going in**: of the 8 JTF projects funded in Wildau to date, **none are hospitality**. They are all industrial, engineering, aerospace, construction. The EHC Tasting House would be the first JTF-funded hospitality fit-out in Wildau. The Reuner template still applies (it sits in the same Landkreis under the same Richtlinie), but ILB caseworkers will be making a slightly new connection. Application narrative needs to do the bridging work.

## Wildau local intelligence: the cluster context

Wildau is not a random Berlin suburb. It is being deliberately built as a Brandenburg innovation hub, with **€34M+ of EU/EFRE money committed to the town in this funding period**. The EHC Tasting House lands as a complementary cultural-hospitality anchor to a wider economic project. That framing strengthens the application.

**Anchors already funded in Wildau**:

- **Reaktor Wildau GmbH** (€2.5M EFRE) — start-up centre under construction now, 2025-2028. Anchor tenants and adjacent commercial space are live questions
- **TH Wildau** (University of Applied Sciences, ~3,500 students) — 50+ EFRE-funded research grants in this period, including a €893k project on **vegan protein co-cultivation**. There is a live food-research thread at the university
- **Fraunhofer-Gesellschaft** — lab footprint in Wildau, multiple EFRE grants
- **ANECOM AeroTest, Nephrolyx, OUR GREENERY, Zell UG, MDI Software** — SME tech and biotech, all EFRE/JTF beneficiaries on site

**Institutional allies worth approaching**:

- **TH Wildau food-research group** (the vegan-protein team) — natural fit for a letter of support, possible joint events ("the science of capsaicin and protein structure"). Worth a single exploratory email
- **Reaktor Wildau GmbH** — adjacent commercial space discussion if their start-up centre needs ground-floor hospitality next door
- **Stadt Wildau Wirtschaftsförderung** — town economic-development office, often writes supporting letters and can flag premises
- **WFBB** (Wirtschaftsförderung Land Brandenburg) — regional layer above, can co-sign cluster-fit framing

**Spatial pitch**: walk out of S-Bahn Wildau station, cross under the railway, you are in the **Schwartzkopff-Siedlung** — 1898 listed industrial-heritage red-brick worker housing built for the locomotive works. TH Wildau campus is 400 m west, the Dahme river is 600 m east. ANECOM AeroTest and the engineering SMEs sit in the Schwartzkopffstraße industrial park to the south. The Tasting House sits in or near the Schwartzkopff quarter: atmosphere, footfall, walkable from station, on the natural axis between university and river.

## Operating model

| Line | What happens | Who pays |
|---|---|---|
| Tasting flights | 5-sauce flight, 45 min, scheduled slots | walk-in + group bookings |
| Pairing dinners | Once-weekly fixed-menu, paired with EHC-Verified makers' sauces | ticketed seats |
| Private hire | Wedding receptions, company events, anniversaries | full venue rental |
| Verified-seal application visits | Maker comes for batch pH testing + induction | maker fee (already in EHC pricing) |
| Retail | EHC-Verified sauces, gift sets, merch | walk-in + e-commerce fulfilment |
| Press / coordination | Photography days for EHC-coordinated events (already a fee line) | already in EHC pricing |

The Verified-seal admin and press work generate cash for EHC already. The venue gives those cash lines a physical home and adds two new ones (tasting and hospitality).

## What is **not** in this budget (and why)

- **Lease deposit and rent**: not eligible, comes out of EHC working capital
- **Land or building purchase**: not eligible
- **Initial sauce stock for retail**: not eligible (used/consumable)
- **Salaries before opening**: not eligible
- **Vehicles**: not eligible
- **Trade fair stands**: not eligible (interesting carve-out, worth knowing)

Plan for these as a **separate own-funded opening budget**, probably €40k-€60k on top, covering 6 months of rent, opening stock, marketing launch, and the first hire's onboarding.

## Lease shape

- **Term**: minimum 5 years, ideally 5+5 with extension option. Covers the 3-year ILB hold period plus a buffer
- **Tenant improvements**: written clause that the fit-out belongs to the tenant (EHC GmbH) and can be removed or retained on landlord's terms at end of lease. Without this, ILB will question whether the asset really sits with the applicant
- **Use clause**: explicit permission for Gastgewerbe (hospitality), Einzelhandel (retail), and Veranstaltungen (events). Don't sign a "Büroflache" lease and try to retrofit
- **Size**: 80-150 sqm covers tasting bar, kitchen, event seating for 40, retail corner, and 20 sqm back office for Verified-seal admin. Plus a terrace
- **Budget signal**: in Wildau, ground-floor commercial space at 80-150 sqm typically sits in €1,500-€3,000/month gross depending on Dahme proximity and Schwartzkopff-quarter premium

## Legal entity

EHC currently has no German GmbH. To apply, the cleanest move is:

- **Form a Brandenburg GmbH** ("EHC Tasting House GmbH" or similar trading name)
- Registered seat **in Wildau**, Landkreis Dahme-Spreewald
- Gesellschafter: ROH or a holding entity owns it, with appropriate licence to use the EHC brand
- Geschäftsführer: Germany-resident, already covered (ROH/EHC principals all reside in Germany)
- Gewerbeanmeldung at the local Gewerbeamt before applying

Lead time on entity formation: 4-8 weeks. Notary appointment plus Handelsregister entry plus tax number plus Gewerbeanmeldung.

## Jobs and "Just Transition" narrative for the application

ILB will want to see a story that fits the JTF purpose: diversifying a coal-transition economy.

Suggested framing:

> The Lausitz region is rebuilding its economy around food tourism, biosphere agriculture, craft production, and innovation clusters as coal capacity winds down. Wildau is at the front of that rebuild, with TH Wildau, Reaktor Wildau and a cohort of engineering and biotech SMEs already drawing public investment. The EHC Tasting House complements that cluster with a cultural-hospitality anchor: a Berlin-reachable destination that positions European craft hot sauce as a recognisable food-culture category, draws day-trippers and visiting researchers, creates year-round hospitality jobs in Wildau, and channels visitor spend to certified makers across the EU.

Job creation, even modest, strengthens the file. Realistic shape at €300k investment scale:

- 1 full-time venue manager
- 2 part-time hospitality staff (weekend / event coverage)
- 1 part-time Verified-seal coordinator (could be remote, but Brandenburg-resident helps)
- Casual event staff on a per-event basis

That is 2.0-2.5 FTE equivalents, plausible for the funding scale.

## Risk flags

- **Berlin reach is solved by Wildau, but only by the S-Bahn**: any disruption on S46/S8/S85 cuts the venue off from its core producer network. Keep this in mind for event-day risk planning
- **De-minimis ceiling shared across linked enterprises**: if ROH and EHC are commonly controlled, their De-minimis pots may be aggregated. Worth a tax adviser check before drawing the org chart
- **Hold period vs lease**: if the lease ends inside 3 years and assets are moved out of the eligible region, ILB can claw back. Lease length is structural, not commercial
- **Verified-seal cross-funding**: the seal scheme is a service revenue line, not the eligible operation. Keep its accounting clear so ILB does not perceive the grant as cross-subsidising consulting work (which is a different track, 2.2, with different rules)
- **AI photo conventions**: existing EHC press uses AI-composite imagery. For the venue, real photography of real people in a real space will be needed for both compliance documentation and the audience. Different content workflow
- **Opening risk**: a permanent venue commits EHC to fixed overhead. Without 60-70% utilisation across tasting + events + retail, it bleeds. Conservative break-even modelling needed before the lease is signed

## Decision points before any move
