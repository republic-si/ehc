A note from the Council
About this catalog.
Edition 01 · A/W 2026

Independent hot sauce in Europe grew up in the last decade the same way craft beer did in the one before it. Farm-side fermenters in Portugal, chilli growers in Poland, table-side sauce houses in the Netherlands and Belgium, small kitchens in Spain and Italy that started as market stalls and ended up on gastro menus. What was missing was a single door for buyers who want the category but do not have time to find every maker one by one.

This catalog is that door. Fourteen producers, seven countries, one cohort. The European Heat Council does not hold stock. Every price and every lead time on the pages that follow is the producer's own, signed off before print. Buyers reach producers directly. The Council routes the introduction, translates where needed, and stays out of the way of the deal.

Inclusion is by curation, not fee. Producers meet a minimum bar on allergen documentation and product liability, and commit to standing behind the MOQs and lead times they publish here. Selection is by house style, category coverage, and reliability. A shorter roster serves buyers better than a longer one.

What you will not find in the pages that follow: private-label offers, promotional pricing, or exclusivity terms. This is a wholesale catalog for buyers who want to build a range around named makers with real stories, and who intend to pay for what those stories are worth.

Buyer guide
How to read a producer page, and how to request a quote.
Anatomy of a page
Producer Name
One-line house style, italicised.
Story
Two short paragraphs. Origin, technique, what the sauces taste of.
Meta grid
Country and city, founding year, house style, EHSA record, IFS status, insurance.
Hero SKUs
SKU name, category, wholesale price band per bottle, MOQ per SKU, lead time from PO.
Compliance flags
Allergen dossier, IFS or BRC, product liability insurance. Filled squares mean confirmed.
Press strip
Selected press mentions. Buyers can request full pickups on demand.
What to check first
1
House style, then category

The style line under the producer name signals fit faster than the SKU list. Read it before scanning prices.

2
MOQ and lead time together

Small producers publish honest lead times. A 6-week lead time on a signature SKU is common and rarely negotiable.

3
Compliance flags for your channel

Grocery buyers need IFS filled. Gastro and Feinkost buyers rarely do. Allergen dossier and liability insurance are the floor.

4
Press strip as tie-breaker

Between two producers you like equally, editorial coverage is the shortest path to a retail buyer's shelf yes.

01
Shortlist

Mark the producers you want quotes from. Any number, any country. There is no cohort minimum.

02
One request

Send one email to catalog@europeanheatcouncil.eu naming the producers, SKUs, and target volumes.

03
Direct quotes

Producers reply to you directly within five working days. Council is copied for coordination only.

Pandemonic grows chilli on a farm in Poland and presses the sauce in Berlin. Karol works a rotating range of cultivars, most of them cooked over live heat before pressing. The finished sauces read closer to a barbecue reduction than to a standard hot sauce, which is why they sell into gastro rather than novelty retail.

Two SKUs are permanent. The rest of the range rotates with the growing year and is announced at each harvest.
