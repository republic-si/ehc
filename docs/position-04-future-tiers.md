# Position 04 — future tier expansions

Captured 2026-06-25. Backlog for the next revision pass of Position 04 (Championing local and regional supply chains).

The current 3-tier framework:

1. **Seed-to-sauce** — producer grows the chilli and makes the sauce
2. **EU-internal sourcing** — all primary ingredients from named EU producers
3. **Mixed traceable** — non-EU ingredients permitted where the ingredient isn't available at meaningful EU scale, all traceable to named producer

Below those tiers: anonymous commodity sourcing, not recognised by the Council.

## Candidate additions for v2

- **"EHC-approved supplier" tag.** When the Council has vetted a supplier (chilli grower, packaging maker, fermenter), downstream producers using that supplier inherit a trust badge. Creates a network effect: as the EHC member base grows, the "EHC-supplier-network" becomes a recognisable trust signal that compounds across the chain.
- **"Independent ingredients" as a separable descriptor.** Distinguishes the ingredients (coming from independent producers) from the sauce maker (also independent). Lets buyers parse the supply chain at two levels rather than collapsing both into a single tier.

## Why hold for v2

- Both require infrastructure the Council does not yet have: a verified supplier list, a member directory, and a vetting standard.
- Shipping the 3-tier framework first lets the Council establish the concept in press and with producers before adding granularity.
- Adding tiers before the Council can verify them risks the framework becoming marketing rather than standard.

## Trigger for revision

Add when:
- The Council has a working member directory (Commitment 2 of Position 04)
- At least one supplier has been formally vetted under a documented standard
- A second producer has been recognised as using that vetted supplier
