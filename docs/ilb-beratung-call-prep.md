# ILB Beratung call

## Purpose

A free 30-60 min phone or video consultation with an ILB programme officer to confirm whether the EHC Tasting House concept, as framed, is fundable under JTF-Unternehmensförderung (Produktive Investitionen von KMU, De-minimis track). Goal: reach a clear "yes the shape works, here's the gap to close" or "no, here's why, here's an alternative".

No commitment is made on this call. ILB cannot approve or reject on a Beratung. They will steer.

## How to book

- Portal: **www.ilb.de**, "Wirtschaft" section, "Beratung anfragen" or "Kundencenter"
- Phone: ILB Kundencenter +49 331 660 1234
- Email: kundencenter@ilb.de
- Ask specifically for **JTF-Unternehmensförderung Beratung**, programme contact **Wirtschaftsförderung Brandenburg / JTF-WS**

Lead time: typically 1-3 weeks. Beratungstermine in German by default, but English is usually available on request. Confirm language when booking.

## Two-line self-introduction

> We are a small Berlin-based food and press business planning to open a permanent tasting and event venue in Wildau under the European Heat Council brand. We want to apply for JTF productive-investment funding for the fit-out, and want to validate the project shape before forming the GmbH.

That signals: serious, prepared, not chancers; pre-application stage; specific track in mind.

## Project one-pager to read out

- **Location**: Wildau, Landkreis Dahme-Spreewald, in or near the Schwartzkopff-Siedlung / TH Wildau / Bahnhof corridor
- **Activity**: permanent tasting room, small event venue, retail of certified European craft hot sauces, with back-office certification administration
- **Sectors (WZ 2008)**: I (Gastgewerbe) primary, G (Einzelhandel) secondary, optionally C (Manufacturing) for gift-set co-packing
- **Vehicle**: new Brandenburg GmbH, registered in Wildau, owned by an existing food-industry holding (ROH), Germany-resident Geschäftsführer
- **Funding ask**: €299,600 total project cost, De-minimis track at 70%, ~€209,720 grant
- **Cost mix**: kitchen, bar, terrace, AV, event kit, POS, signage, branded fit-out, lab corner for pH testing, planning fees
- **Jobs**: 2.0-2.5 FTE plus event casuals, year-round
- **Strategic fit**: complements TH Wildau and Reaktor Wildau as a cultural-hospitality anchor in a transition-region innovation cluster

## Questions to ask, in order

Open with the high-leverage ones. If you only get 20 minutes, the first three matter most.

1. **"Is the project as described fundable under JTF-Unternehmensförderung, Maßnahme 2.1, De-minimis track?"** (the yes/no question that justifies the call)
2. **"Are the cost line items we listed all eligible under this Maßnahme, or would any of them need to be excluded or re-categorised?"** (catches the kitchen/bar/AV/lab-corner mix; the answer tells you the real budget)
3. **"For a new GmbH that has not yet been founded, what is the earliest point we can submit a Vorhabenantrag?"** (does the entity need to exist already, or can we file in formation? In-formation submission saves 4-8 weeks)
4. "Does our combination of hospitality + retail + pH-testing lab corner sit cleanly inside Maßnahme 2.1, or does the lab corner trigger a different track or special review?"
5. "Are there sector restrictions inside WZ 56 (Gastronomie) we should know about? Anything common that gets rejected?"
6. "The €299,600 total cost matches the €300,000 De-minimis ceiling. Are there reasons to stay further below the ceiling, or is at-cap a normal application shape?"
7. "Our principals also operate ROH, a separate food business, in Germany. If both companies are under common ownership, how does De-minimis aggregation work? Do we need to disclose ROH's De-minimis history on the EHC application?"
8. "Is there a recommended minimum lease term that ILB looks for, beyond the 3-year asset-hold rule? Anything explicit in the Richtlinie or only convention?"
9. "How long does a complete JTF-Unternehmensförderung application typically take from submission to Zuwendungsbescheid?"
10. "Can you point us at any **comparable funded projects** in Wildau or LDS we could use as reference? We have studied the public Vorhabenliste."
11. "Would a letter of support from **TH Wildau** or **Stadt Wildau Wirtschaftsförderung** materially strengthen the application?"
12. "Are there parallel programmes from MWAEK, GRW, or Brandenburg's tourism budget that could complement (not double-fund) the same project?"
13. "Who at ILB will be our point of contact through the application?" (gets a name and email for follow-up)

## Things to listen for

- **Any hesitation around hospitality in Wildau specifically.** The dataset shows no hospitality JTF projects in Wildau yet. If the officer steers toward Spreewald instead, hear them out. They know things the dataset doesn't.
- **Pressure to use the AGVO track instead of De-minimis.** AGVO unlocks higher project cost but at lower co-finance for LDS (D-Area, 20% small enterprise rate). For €300k project size, De-minimis is the smarter shape. Resist a steer to AGVO unless the project genuinely needs to be larger.
- **"You need to also apply through GRW / WFBB / Tourismus Marketing Brandenburg."** Not a red flag, but worth understanding before agreeing to expand the application surface. Each parallel track adds months.
- **Comments about how busy the programme is.** As of August 2025, 1,000+ JTF applications had been filed. Capacity pressure is real. Officer's tone here matters.
- **Anything about Verwendungsnachweis complexity for first-time applicants.** This is the post-award reporting burden. If they emphasise it, expect significant administrative load.

## Red flags that should change the plan

- "Hospitality in Wildau is unusual for this Maßnahme; we usually steer this kind of project to Maßnahme X" — likely means a different programme is a better fit, follow the steer
- "Your lab corner and tasting bar combination triggers a different review track" — may need to drop one or split the project
- "De-minimis aggregation across ROH and EHC will exhaust your ceiling" — need to restructure the corporate group before applying
- "The Wildau location does not have the strategic fit profile we're prioritising" — pivot to a Spreewald site or accept a lower probability of approval

## After the call

Within 24 hours:

- Write up the officer's answers under each question above, verbatim where possible
- Update `ehc-tasting-house-concept.md` to reflect any corrections to scope, location, or sequencing
- Decide go / no-go on entity formation
- If go: book notary appointment for GmbH founding
- If no-go: park the concept doc and consider the verified-maker partnering alternative

## Two practical notes

- **Take the call from a quiet room with the Vorhabenliste open in another tab.** The officer will assume you have read it. Being able to refer to specific funded projects by name (Reuner Denise, Kuhla Nadine, Café Kreuztor) signals you have done the homework
- **Have the JTF-Unternehmensförderung Richtlinie open too.** Section numbers help: "Maßnahme 2.1", "Artikel 6 Absatz 2 AGVO", "Anlage 1 zur Richtlinie". Speaking in the right paragraph references shifts the conversation from "we'll see" to "let's check the rule"
