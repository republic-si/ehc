# JTF Brandenburg — funding fit for EHC

## TL;DR

A real, live EU programme funds the exact thing EHC might want to do: take a small physical space in a transition region, fit it out as a tasting / certification / event venue, and get most of the bill covered. Headline: **up to 70% co-finance, project cap €299,600, grant ceiling ~€210,000 per applicant per 3 years**. The funding body is the **Investitionsbank des Landes Brandenburg (ILB)**. The catch is geography: the venue must sit in the **Lausitzer Revier** (Cottbus, Dahme-Spreewald, Elbe-Elster, Oberspreewald-Lausitz, Spree-Neiße) or the **Uckermark**. Berlin is out.

The plaque at Stork Club is one instance of this programme. The dataset shows roughly 50 SME hospitality / event / venue fit-outs funded under the same line, with about a dozen hitting the €299,600 cap at the same 70% rate.

## The programme

- Name: **JTF-Unternehmensförderung** (Just Transition Fund — SME business funding)
- Issuing ministry: MWAEK Brandenburg
- Administered by: **ILB** (Investitionsbank des Landes Brandenburg)
- Legal base: Richtlinie 2023, amended 27.06.2024; Uckermark variant Richtlinie 2025
- Programme window: 11.08.2023 to 30.06.2027 (rolling submission, no application deadline)
- Track that matches EHC: **2.1 Produktive Investitionen von KMU**, De-minimis variant

## Headline numbers

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Co-financing rate (De-minimis track) | up to **70%** of eligible costs |
| Project cost cap (De-minimis track) | **€300,000** total |
| Maximum grant per applicant per 3 years | **€300,000** (EU De-minimis ceiling) |
| Minimum project size (De-minimis) | €10,000 |
| Own contribution required | minimum 25%, no other public money on the same euros |
| Hold period (assets must remain in region) | 3 years (KMU) |

The dataset shows the working pattern: project priced at €299,600, grant ≈ €209,720, own contribution ≈ €89,880.

## Geographic eligibility

Hard constraint. The applicant must have **either headquarters or at least one Betriebsstätte (operating site, branch, or office)** inside the eligible region, at the time of grant and for the full duration of the project plus the 3-year hold period.

Eligible regions:

- **Lausitzer Revier** (covered by Richtlinie 2023):
  - Stadt Cottbus
  - Landkreis Dahme-Spreewald (**includes Wildau, Königs Wusterhausen, Eichwalde, Zeuthen, Schönefeld — all in the Berlin S-Bahn commuter belt**, plus Schlepzig, Lübben, Lübbenau in the Spreewald further south)
  - Landkreis Elbe-Elster
  - Landkreis Oberspreewald-Lausitz
  - Landkreis Spree-Neiße
- **Landkreis Uckermark** (covered by separate 2025 Richtlinie, same mechanics)

Berlin itself and the rest of Brandenburg outside these areas are not eligible. The Dahme-Spreewald boundary reaches up to Berlin's southern edge, so a JTF-eligible venue can sit one S-Bahn ride from central Berlin (e.g. Wildau on S46, ~40 min from Berlin Westend).

## Who can apply

Eligible by sector (WZ 2008):

- **C** Manufacturing — covers food/drink production, including sauce making
- **G** Trade — covers retail
- **I** Hospitality (Gastgewerbe) — covers restaurants, bars, tasting rooms, event venues
- **J** Information and communication
- **M 71.1** Architecture and engineering
- **N** Other business services
- **S 95, S 96.01-96.02** Repair, laundry, cosmetics

Excluded: agriculture, fisheries, fossil energy, finance, telecoms infrastructure, water utilities, care homes, tobacco.

Size: SME (under 250 employees, ≤ €50M revenue or ≤ €43M balance sheet).

Legal form: any registered commercial entity ("gewerbliche Wirtschaft"). Sole traders, GbR, UG, GmbH all show up in the live dataset.

**Implication for EHC**: a Verein or non-commercial entity is **not** eligible. EHC would need to act through a commercial vehicle, e.g. a small German GmbH or UG operating the venue as a hospitality or manufacturing business. Either it registers itself as such, or it pairs with a maker who is already an SME in the region.

## Eligible costs

The Reuner project description is a clean template of what the programme accepts:

- Building conversion and fit-out
- Fixed fittings (terrace, bar, kitchen)
- Furniture and equipment (seating, dishes, cutlery)
- Event and hospitality equipment (tents, fire bowls, AV, signage)
- Mobile point-of-sale systems and digital displays
- Machinery and tools tied to the operation
- Software and licences tied to the investment
- Planning and design fees directly linked to the build

Explicitly **not** eligible:

- Land purchase
- Vehicles for primary transport
- Used goods
- Routine repairs and maintenance
- Internal consulting or family-member advice
- Trade fair participation
- Recoverable VAT
- BHKW and PV systems (out under this track)
- Financing costs, leases, rentals

Reimbursement model: ILB pays only against documented, paid invoices submitted after the work is done (Erstattungsprinzip). Cash flow is on the applicant until invoices clear.

## Application mechanics

- Portal: **www.ilb.de**, online application
- Timing rule, hard: the full written application must be filed **before any work on the project begins**. Once ILB confirms receipt, the applicant may start at own risk, but rejection then means no money.
- No fixed window. Rolling submission until 30.06.2027.
- De-minimis declaration required: list all De-minimis aid received in the previous two tax years plus the current year, capped at €300,000 cumulative.
- AGVO Article 6(2) content requirements: project plan, cost breakdown, financing plan, no-relocation confirmation (Art. 2(61a) AGVO), own-contribution proof.
- Post-award: Verwendungsnachweis (usage report) under ANBest-EU 21, retention of evidence, transparency disclosure to EU database from 01.01.2026 for De-minimis cases.

## Fit assessment for EHC

What works in EHC's favour:

- Hospitality sector (I) and food manufacturing (C) are both explicitly eligible
- A tasting room, certification HQ, or branded venue maps cleanly to funded examples in the dataset
- Berlin proximity is good: Schlepzig is 60 km from Berlin, Cottbus 125 km, Schwedt 95 km. A satellite venue in Lausitz or Uckermark is plausible operationally
- Rolling submission, no deadline pressure

What blocks it today:

- EHC is not currently constituted as a German commercial entity in an eligible region
- Without a German SME registration with Sitz or Betriebsstätte in Lausitz or Uckermark, no application
- The Verified seal programme as currently scoped is a service, not a fixed-asset investment, so it would not itself be the eligible operation. The eligible operation needs to be physical: building, fit-out, equipment

## Possible paths

1. **EHC pairs with a verified maker already in the region.** The maker applies for fit-out funding for a tasting-room / showroom build that EHC programmes and brands. EHC contributes content, the maker owns the asset. Lowest legal lift, depends on a willing partner.
2. **EHC sets up a Brandenburg GmbH or UG** with a registered Betriebsstätte in an eligible Landkreis. The GmbH operates an EHC-branded venue (tasting room, event space, certification HQ). It applies in its own name. Needs entity formation, lease, local registration, and an operating plan that reads as a real hospitality or manufacturing business.
3. **EHC acquires a small existing SME in the region and rebrands.** Higher cost, but inherits the eligible legal entity and operating history. Most defensible if the acquired SME already runs comparable activity.

## Open questions before any move

- Is there a real EHC venue ambition this calendar year, or is this a 2027+ idea? The programme runs to 30.06.2027 but applications need lead time.
- Which region fits the EHC narrative best: Spreewald (food and biosphere story, near Berlin) or Uckermark (event-infrastructure track record, slightly further out)?
- Is there an existing maker who would partner on Path 1?
- What is the smallest viable EHC physical footprint that still reads as a real venue to ILB? A 50 sqm tasting room with a kitchen and event capacity probably qualifies.
